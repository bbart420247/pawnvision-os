How It Works

Remote intake becomes broker-ready intelligence in a few steps.

Simple enough for a pawn shop owner to understand immediately. Structured enough for multi-location operations to scale.

Step 1

Customer submits item remotely

The customer uploads an item photo, adds a short description, and starts the intake before visiting the shop.

Remote submission Photo evidence + short description
Step 2

AI organizes item details and evidence

PawnVision OS identifies the likely category, checks what is visible in the photos, and requests better angles when the evidence is incomplete.

Evidence organization Category + visible condition + missing proof
Step 3

System pulls market comps and sold data

The broker-facing report organizes preliminary value support, comp sources, and proof links when reliable market data is available.

Market support layer Sold comps + active listings + retail context
Step 4

Broker reviews the intelligence

The AI does not make the final decision. It gives the broker a faster starting point, and the broker adjusts, approves, rejects, or asks for more proof.

Broker decision layer Approve + adjust + reject + request more
Step 5

Item gets tagged and tracked in smart inventory

Once accepted, the item can move into structured inventory workflows instead of disappearing into paper tags, browser tabs, and disconnected notes.

Inventory handoff Tagging + tracking + location-ready workflow
PawnVision OS

From intake to inventory, the platform is built to help brokers decide faster.
